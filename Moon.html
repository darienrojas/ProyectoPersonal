    THE MOON

    In the year 2065, Deep Nebula, the most influential corporation in space exploration, made history by bringing the first civilians to the Moon through its Lunar Horizon Program. This ambitious initiative not only marked a milestone in space exploration but also democratized access to space, allowing ordinary people to experience what was once reserved only for highly trained astronauts.

    By 2070, Deep Nebula had transported over 10,000 civilians to the Moon, cementing its role as a pioneer in making space accessible to all. The company's vision extended beyond the Moon, with plans to establish similar programs for Mars and beyond.

    1972

    The only instances of humans living on the Moon have taken place in an Apollo Lunar Module for several days at a time (Apollo 17)

    2019

    At least one plant seed sprouted in an experiment on the Chang'e 4 lander. It was carried from Earth along with other small life in its Lunar Micro Ecosystem.

    2055

    NASA's Artemis program achieved a major milestone in 2023 with the establishment of the Artemis Base Camp near the Moon's south pole.

    2070

    By 2070, the Moon had become a fully integrated extension of human civilization, with lunar cities, advanced research facilities, and a booming off-world economy. At the heart of this transformation was Deep Nebula, a powerful megacorporation that emerged as the dominant force in lunar and space exploration.
